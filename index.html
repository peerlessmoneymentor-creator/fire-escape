📚 What is a pension — and how is it different?

A pension (Defined Benefit plan) promises you a fixed monthly income for life in retirement, calculated by your employer based on years of service and salary. You don't control the investments — your employer does.

A 401(k) or IRA (Defined Contribution) gives you an account where you invest your own money. The final balance depends entirely on what you contributed and how the market performed. You control it — and own it.

🏛️ Pension pros
• Guaranteed income for life
• Employer bears investment risk
• Predictable — great for budgeting
• Often includes survivor benefits
⚠️ Pension cons
• Not portable — tied to employer
• No lump sum to pass to heirs
• May reduce if you leave early
• You can't invest it yourself

💡 The key insight: Even with a pension, you can and should still build personal wealth through a Roth IRA, brokerage account, and HSA. Your pension covers baseline income — personal investing builds freedom and a legacy.
